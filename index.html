  • We are not able to represent you under CLAS until you have been charged in court. Please submit your application after you have been charged in court.

  • What is being charged in court?

    Being charged in court is when you appear in court for the first time and what crime is thought that you have committed is read out to you in front of a judge. You should also be given a copy of a Charge Sheet, which generally looks like this:

    In order to apply for CLAS, you need a copy of this Charge Sheet.

  • We generally are only able to represent you under the Criminal Legal Aid Scheme (CLAS) if you have one or more charge covered in our List of Statutes.

    If you believe you have an exceptional case, please email help@probono.sg.

    Alternatively, if you are from a government agency, social service agency or other legal aid providers and your client has exhausted all other legal avenues, you may refer your client to us by emailing us at ahpbs@probono.sg.

  • These are the statutes of offences covered under the Criminal Legal Aid Scheme (CLAS):

    • Arms & Explosives Act (Cap. 13)
    • Arms Offences Act (Cap. 14)
    • Computer Misuse Act (Cap. 50A)
    • Corrosive & Explosive Substances & Offensive Weapons Act (Cap. 65)
    • Dangerous Fireworks Act (Cap. 72)
    • Enlistment Act (Cap. 93)
    • Explosive Substances Act (Cap. 100)
    • Films Act (Cap. 107)
    • Miscellaneous Offences (Public Order and Nuisance) Act (Cap. 184)
    • Misuse of Drugs Act (Cap. 185)
    • Moneylender's Act (Cap. 188) [Sections 19 & 47]
    • Penal Code (Cap. 224)
    • Prevention of Corruption Act (Cap. 241)
    • Undesirable Publications Act (Cap. 338)
    • Vandalism Act (Cap. 341)
    • Women’s Charter (Cap. 353) [Sections 65(8) and 140(1)(i)]

    If you have multiple charges, please select "yes" if one or more of them are charges under these statutes.

  • We are not able to represent you under CLAS if you already have a lawyer on file.

    • If you are looking to have your current lawyer represent you under CLAS, please get your lawyer to write in to clas@probono.sg.
    • If you have a lawyer but wish to make an application for another lawyer under CLAS, please discharge your previous lawyer first. Do note however that you need to be financially needy to qualify for CLAS.

    We are sorry to hear about the difficult situation.

    • If you are in immediate danger, please contact the police:
      • Call 999 / 1800 255 0000
      • SMS 71999 if it is not safe for you to talk.
      • Other police contact information here
    • If you are facing a medical emergency, please call the ambulance (995).
    • If you are in emotional distress, you may:
      • Call Samaritans of Singapore’s 24-hour hotline (1-767)
      • WhatsApp Samaritans of Singapore (+65 9151 1767)
    • If you would like to report violence or abuse, you may call the National Anti-Violence and Sexual Harassment Helpline (NAVH) at 1800-777-0000 (24-hour hotline) or make an online report.
    • If you would like to file for a personal protection order, you may refer to our FAQs here. A guide on filing a protection order can also be found on Solid Ground's website.
    • If you would like to file a protection order against a family member, the following organisations can help you with filing a protection order:
      • Family Justice Courts' Family Protection Centre - 3 Havelock Square, Singapore 059725 (Monday to Friday, 9am-12pm)
      • TRANS Safe Centre (Call 6449 9088, Monday – Friday 9:00am-5:00 pm)
      • Care Corner (Call 6476 1482, weekdays 10am – 1pm, 2pm – 5pm; except public holidays))

    If you need any other kind of help that you can't find on this list, please send us an email at help@probono.sg. We'll do our best to connect you with someone who can help you out.

  • What legal help can I get?

  • FROM PRO BONO SG

    • For your criminal law issue that you wish to get a lawyer to represent you for, you can apply for Pro Bono SG's Criminal Legal Aid Scheme (CLAS). If your application is successful, CLAS will assign you a pro bono lawyer to take on your criminal case.
    • For your family law issue that you wish to get a lawyer to represent you for, you can apply for help under Pro Bono SG's Family Justice Support Scheme (FJSS) (Modest Means Clearinghouse). If your application is successful, FJSS will refer you to up to 3 lawyers who agree to charge fees lower than their usual rates, depending on your financial circumstances and the facts of your case.
    • For your family law issue that you wish to get a lawyer to represent you for, you can apply for help under Pro Bono SG's Family Justice Support Scheme (FJSS) (Foreign Spouses). If your application is successful, FJSS will assign you a pro bono lawyer or refer you to up to 3 lawyers who agree to charge fees lower than their usual rates, depending on your financial circumstances and the facts of your case.
    • It appears you are not currently eligible for Pro Bono SG's representation schemes (where you are assigned a lawyer to help with your case). You are encouraged to apply to the Legal Aid Bureau instead (contact details below).
    • You can still register for our Legal Clinics to get one-off legal guidance from a lawyer for free by completing the application form on the next page.
    • If you have been rejected from the Legal Aid Bureau but really need help with your case, please write in to help@probono.sg.
    • Alternatively, if you are from a government agency, social service agency or other legal aid providers and your client has exhausted all other legal avenues, you may refer your client to us by emailing us at ahpbs@probono.sg.
    • If you wish to talk to a lawyer about your problem, you can apply for help under Pro Bono SG's Legal Clinics. During a legal clinic, a lawyer will provide you with one-off legal guidance for free.
